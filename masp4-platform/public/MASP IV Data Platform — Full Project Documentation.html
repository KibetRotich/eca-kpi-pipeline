1. Project Overview

The MASP IV Data Platform is a web-based monitoring and results management system built for Solidaridad East & Central Africa (ECA) to track, aggregate, and visualise programme results under the MASP IV strategy (2026–2030).

The platform replaces manual spreadsheet reporting and CSV-based static dashboards with a live database-backed system. It covers seven outcome KPIs and one output KPI across projects in Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, and Ethiopia, spanning commodities including Coffee, Tea, F&V, Gold, Dairy, Palm Oil, Cocoa, and others.

Programme

MASP IV — Solidaridad East & Central Africa

Period: 2026–2030  ·  Baseline year: 2026

Countries: Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Ethiopia

Platform URL

Production: masp4-platform.vercel.app
GitHub: github.com/KibetRotich/masp4-platform

Target domain: masp4.solidaridadnetwork.org

Design Principles

  • Sample-based extrapolation for farmer-level outcome KPIs (S6.1, S6.2, S2.1)
  • Direct counting for institutional and company KPIs (S2.5, S6.3, S6.4, S6.5)
  • Direct headcount for the output KPI (Farmers Trained / Reached — quarterly)
  • 2026 is baseline only — no extrapolation applied; achievements calculated from 2027 onwards
  • Role-based access: M&E Officers edit, all others view
  • All approved data is immutable once in the KPI views

4. KPI Framework

CodeIndicatorPathwayMethodWhat is counted
S6.1 Farmers with enhanced resilience Production Sample + Extrapolation Farmers whose Resilience Index (0–35) meets or exceeds the threshold (≥18, provisional). Index sums 7 sub-scores: soil health, collective membership, local decision-making, income stability, shock recovery, savings buffer, income diversification.
S6.2 Farmers with improved farm viability Production Sample + Extrapolation Farmers whose Farm Viability Index (0–30) exceeds 33% threshold (score >10). Index sums 6 sub-scores: yield growth, income diversification, income perception, service quality (NPS proxy), market access, GAP adoption.
S2.1 Farmers accessing new/improved services Services Sample + Extrapolation Farmers who received ≥1 new service OR whose service quality improved. Confirmed by SP triangulation.
S2.5 Individuals co-owning businesses Services Direct Count Direct headcount of farmers and SPs holding recognised co-ownership stakes. No extrapolation.
S6.3 Regulations / frameworks improved Governance Direct Count Mandatory regulations or voluntary frameworks improved, established, or implemented. Scored on Tier 1/2/3 scale by CSO respondents.
S6.4 Companies rewarding farmers directly Market Direct Count Partner companies that have implemented direct farmer reward mechanisms (premium payments, bonus schemes). Reported by company respondents.
S6.5 Companies with responsible procurement Market Direct Count Partner companies scored on 5 responsible procurement criteria: policy document, SMART time-bound commitments, action plan, country coverage, third-party verification.
OUT.1 Farmers trained / reached (Output KPI) Cross-cutting Direct Count (Quarterly) Total headcount of farmers trained or reached through all delivery channels: training events, TV/radio, demo farms, digital platforms. Collected quarterly per project.
Note on S6.1, S6.2, S2.1: These three KPIs survey the same farmers in a single combined interview. They are independent outcome measures — do not sum or average them. The Output KPI (OUT.1) is a completely separate headcount from the outcome surveys.

5. Data Collection & Extrapolation Methodology

Farmer Survey Cycle

1
Define target population
Project team enters the total target population in Targets & Achievements — total, female, and male — for each KPI and each survey year.
2
Draw sample
A random or systematic sample is drawn. Gender-stratified sampling is recommended so that female and male farmers are sampled proportionally.
3
Administer survey
Enumerators use the MASP IV Farmer Survey V1.1 (XLSForm) on KoboToolbox mobile devices. One interview covers S6.1, S6.2, S2.1, and S2.5.
4
Export CSV from KoboToolbox
Project officer exports the completed survey as CSV from KoboToolbox (Data → Downloads → CSV, all fields).
5
Upload to platform
CSV uploaded via Import CSV tab. Rows enter a pending queue. M&E Officer reviews and approves each submission.
6
Extrapolation applied
On approval, the KPI views automatically calculate extrapolated achievement using the post-stratification estimator (from 2027 onwards).

Post-Stratification Estimator

For S6.1, S6.2, and S2.1, the number of farmers meeting the threshold in the full target population is estimated as follows:

Formula A — Gender-stratified (preferred)
Achievement = (f_threshold / f_surveyed) × Target_Female
            + (m_threshold / m_surveyed) × Target_Male
Formula B — Total only (when gender targets not available)
Achievement = (sample_count / sample_size) × Target_Total
TermDefinition
f_thresholdFemale farmers in sample who meet the KPI threshold
f_surveyedTotal female farmers surveyed
m_threshold / m_surveyedSame for male
Target_Female / Target_MaleGender-disaggregated target population (from Targets & Achievements)
sample_countTotal farmers in sample meeting the threshold (for Formula B)
Baseline year (2026): No extrapolation is applied. Raw sample counts are displayed as reference values. The estimator activates from 2027, comparing against 2026 baseline scores.

Threshold Definitions

KPIThresholdNotes
S6.1Resilience Index ≥ 18 / 35Provisional — to be confirmed after 2026 baseline collection
S6.2Viability Index > 10 / 30 (>33%)Per MASP IV monitoring protocol
S2.1≥1 new service received OR quality improvedConfirmed by SP triangulation

Collective membership5f_S61_membership (0–5)

Soil C:N scoring: ratio 10–15 = 5pts, 15–20 or 8–10 = 4pts, 20–25 or 5–8 = 3pts, 25–30 or 3–5 = 2pts, >30 or <3 = 1pt.
Shock resilience: impact × recovery product (2–15) recategorised: ≤4=5, ≤6=4, ≤9=3, ≤12=2, else=1.

8. Output KPI: Farmers Trained / Reached

This is a direct headcount output indicator — separate from the 7 outcome KPIs. It counts all farmers reached across all delivery channels per project per quarter.

Delivery channels included

  • Face-to-face training events
  • TV / radio broadcasts
  • Demonstration farms
  • Digital platforms

How it is collected

  • M&E Officer enters Q1–Q4 counts per project in Targets & Achievements
  • Annual achievement = Q1 + Q2 + Q3 + Q4 (auto-summed)
  • Annual target stored in project_kpi_targets as kpi_code = 'OUT.1'
  • Dashboard banner shows the programme-wide total

Database

Quarterly records stored in project_output_records (unique per project × year × quarter). Annual target stored in project_kpi_targets under code OUT.1.

14. Operational Procedures

Adding a new project

1
Run INSERT in Supabase SQL Editor
Use pipeline/masp4_seed_projects_2026.sql as template. Required: project_code (unique), project_name, country, commodity, start_year, end_year.
2
Verify commodity enum
Commodity must be a registered enum value. If new: ALTER TYPE commodity_enum ADD VALUE IF NOT EXISTS 'NewCommodity';
3
Confirm in platform
Reload Targets & Achievements — new project card appears. Check country and commodity filters work correctly.
4
Enter annual KPI targets
Click + Add next to each of the 7 outcome KPIs. Enter target total + female/male disaggregation. Repeat for each survey year.
5
Enter output KPI annual target
In the yellow OUTPUT section, click + Add target. Enter planned annual headcount across all delivery channels.
6
Enter quarterly output actuals
At end of each quarter, enter Q1–Q4 counts. Annual achievement sums automatically with % vs target.

Correcting a project's commodity or country

Run a targeted UPDATE in Supabase SQL Editor. Create a fix file in pipeline/ for audit trail:

UPDATE projects SET commodity = 'F&V' WHERE project_code = 'KE-ANK-001';

Promoting a user to M&E Officer

After the user has signed in at least once via Google:

UPDATE user_roles SET role = 'me_officer' WHERE email = 'colleague@solidaridadnetwork.org';
